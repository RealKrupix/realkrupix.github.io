Prof. Bernadetta Janusz

email: bernadetta.e.janusz@gmail.com

Dr hab. Bernadetta Janusz is a clinical psychologist and anthropologist. She is currently an associate professor at the Chair of Psychiatry, Medical College, Jagiellonian University in Kraków, Poland. She works as an individual, couple, and family psychotherapist. Bernadetta completed her psychodynamic and systemic training in the School of Family Therapy, accredited by the Polish Psychiatric Association, in 2002. lead by prof Bogdan de Barbaro She completed training in Psychoanalytical Psychotherapy in 2019 at the School of Olga Pilinow, accredited by the Polish Society of Psychoanalytical Psychotherapy. She has been a certified psychotherapist since 2003 and has been accredited as a supervisor of psychotherapy by the Polish Psychiatric Association since 2010. She specialized in Clinical Psychology in 2010 and works as a trainer of couple and family therapy. Having received additional training from the Anna Freud Centre and the Portland Institute of Loss and Transition, she is particularly interested in mentalization in clinical practice as well as treating complicated grief and prolonged grief disorder. Bernadetta's research focuses on loss-related processes in the family, grieving, and family therapy interactions. In her studies, she has combined different qualitative methods, such as conversation analysis and interpersonal process recall.
